# How to Take the Perfect Photo for an AI Pet Portrait

The AI that generates your dog's portrait is only as good as the photo you give it. A clear, well-lit reference produces a portrait with a strong likeness. A blurry or obscured photo produces a portrait that looks like a generic dog in a costume.

Here's what separates a great input photo from a poor one.

## The Most Important Thing: Face Visibility

The portrait is centered on your dog's face. The AI needs to see it clearly — both eyes, the full muzzle, ears, and any distinctive markings. If part of the face is hidden, in shadow, or blurred, that information won't make it into the portrait.

**Do:** Photos where your dog is looking at the camera or slightly toward it.

**Avoid:** Side profiles, photos where the dog is looking away, or shots where the face is partially obscured by a toy, another dog, or your hand.

## Lighting Makes a Huge Difference

Natural light is your best friend. The model reads color and texture from your photo — harsh artificial lighting or heavy shadows change how the fur color reads and can wash out detail.

**Best:** Outdoors in soft natural light (overcast days are ideal — no harsh shadows).

**Good:** Indoors near a window in daylight.

**Avoid:** Flash photography (creates flat, washed-out color), heavy shadows across the face, backlighting that silhouettes your dog.

## Background: Simple Wins

A busy background — other dogs, people, furniture, patterns — pulls the model's attention away from your dog. A simple background keeps the focus on the subject.

**Best:** Solid colored backgrounds, grass, plain walls.

**Avoid:** Crowds, cluttered rooms, other pets in frame.

## Resolution and Sharpness

Modern smartphones take more than enough resolution for great portraits. The bigger concern is sharpness — a photo taken while your dog is mid-movement will be blurry, and blurry inputs produce blurry outputs.

**Tips:**
- Wait for your dog to be still before shooting
- Tap your dog's face on the phone screen to focus on them specifically
- Take multiple photos — you'll have better options to choose from

## Angle: Front-Facing Is Best

The AI generates a forward-facing portrait. The closer your input is to a front-facing angle, the stronger the likeness in the output. A slight angle is fine. A sharp profile is not.

## Quick Checklist

Before uploading, check your photo against this list:

- [ ] Face fully visible, both eyes showing
- [ ] Looking at or toward the camera
- [ ] Taken in good natural or window light
- [ ] No heavy shadows across the face
- [ ] Dog is the clear subject, simple background
- [ ] Photo is sharp, not blurry
- [ ] Front-facing or slight angle

If you can check all of these, you're going to get a strong portrait.
