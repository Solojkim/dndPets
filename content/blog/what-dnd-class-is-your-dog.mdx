# What DnD Class Is Your Dog?

Every dog has a personality. Some are noble and loyal, always at your side. Others are chaotic energy machines who have never once followed a rule. A few are quiet observers who clearly know something you don't.

In Dungeons & Dragons, those same archetypes map onto character classes. And once you see it, you can't unsee it.

Here's a breakdown of all 8 DnD classes — and the type of dog that belongs in each one.

## Paladin — The Loyal Protector

The Paladin is devoted, disciplined, and driven by a deep sense of duty. They show up. They follow through. They would take a hit for you without blinking.

**Your dog is a Paladin if:** They follow you room to room. They alert you when something's wrong. They greet every guest with calm confidence, not frantic barking. They have never once let you down.

Breeds that lean Paladin: Golden Retriever, Labrador, German Shepherd.

## Barbarian — Pure Chaotic Energy

The Barbarian doesn't think. The Barbarian *acts*. Loud, bold, completely unfiltered — they charge into every situation at full speed and figure out the damage later.

**Your dog is a Barbarian if:** They zoom around the house for no reason. They've destroyed at least one couch cushion. They go absolutely feral when you grab the leash. They are *very* excited about everything, all the time.

Breeds that lean Barbarian: Husky, Jack Russell Terrier, Boxer.

## Wizard — The Analytical Thinker

The Wizard is always observing, always calculating. High intelligence, independent streak, and a talent for solving problems you didn't know existed.

**Your dog is a Wizard if:** They figure out how to open doors. They watch you with unsettling focus. They learn commands instantly but only follow them when they feel like it. You sometimes wonder who's actually training who.

Breeds that lean Wizard: Border Collie, Poodle, Australian Shepherd.

## Ranger — The Quiet Observer

The Ranger is patient, methodical, and self-reliant. They don't need your attention every second. They're watching, waiting, always aware of their environment.

**Your dog is a Ranger if:** They're content doing their own thing. They notice things before you do. They're calm in new environments. They have a specific spot they always return to — their personal watch post.

Breeds that lean Ranger: Vizsla, Weimaraner, Basenji.

## Rogue — The Mysterious One

The Rogue moves quietly and acts on instinct. They're bonded to the people they trust, invisible to everyone else. They always seem to know something you don't.

**Your dog is a Rogue if:** They appear out of nowhere. They steal food silently and without remorse. They are suspiciously well-behaved around strangers and save the chaos for home. They have multiple hiding spots you haven't found yet.

Breeds that lean Rogue: Shiba Inu, Whippet, Basenji.

## Cleric — The Gentle Healer

The Cleric is gentle, devoted, and attuned to the emotions of everyone around them. They sense when you're sad before you've said a word.

**Your dog is a Cleric if:** They come to you when you're upset without being called. They're extraordinarily gentle with children. They're the peacekeeper when other dogs play too rough. They have healing powers — specifically, making bad days better.

Breeds that lean Cleric: Cavalier King Charles Spaniel, Saint Bernard, Newfoundland.

## Druid — The Nature Spirit

The Druid is earthy, intuitive, and deeply connected to the natural world. They follow their instincts. They are most alive outdoors.

**Your dog is a Druid if:** They lose their mind in the woods. They sniff every single blade of grass. They're fascinated by weather, bugs, and things you can't smell. They seem ancient and wise in ways you can't fully explain.

Breeds that lean Druid: Irish Setter, Bloodhound, Bernese Mountain Dog.

## Bard — The Life of the Party

The Bard is magnetic, social, and impossible to ignore. Their energy is infectious. Everyone loves them. They know it.

**Your dog is a Bard if:** Every stranger on the street wants to pet them. They perform for attention and are *very good at it*. They have range — playful, affectionate, dramatic when ignored. The room changes when they enter it.

Breeds that lean Bard: Corgi, Beagle, Pomeranian.
